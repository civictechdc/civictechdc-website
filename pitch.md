Civic Tech DC is rooted in the Washington, DC, Maryland, and Virginia community, but a project can serve people elsewhere when it has a clear public benefit and the right partners. Nonprofits, legal clinics, journalists, academics, researchers, community groups, community members, public-interest staff, and civic technologists can all bring a need.

Selected projects receive **no-cost, volunteer-led discovery, technical consultation, data help, product scoping, design, and open-source development support**. This is a collaborative project model, not guaranteed on-demand consulting or a promise that every proposal will ship.

## What support may include

Depending on the project and available volunteers, Civic Tech DC may help with:

- interviewing users and testing assumptions;
- finding a validating organization, community member, or subject-matter expert;
- product strategy, technical consultation, and a manageable first scope;
- data management, analysis, visualization, or reproducible data workflows;
- user experience, software design, and open-source development;
- volunteer recruitment, project coordination, documentation, and handoff planning.

We build **with** problem owners and users, not for an absent client. Useful projects need people with lived or professional knowledge to make decisions, review work, and help sustain what comes next.

## What makes a project a fit

You do not need every answer before the first email. These are the conditions we work toward before forming a project team.

## What we need from a project partner

A problem owner, organization, or validating partner should be prepared to:

- explain the current workflow and connect the team with people who use it;
- attend regular check-ins and respond to questions between meetings;
- review prototypes, data definitions, and product decisions;
- help resolve privacy, security, policy, and subject-matter questions;
- identify who can own, maintain, or responsibly retire the work after launch;
- allow reusable technical work to be developed in the open unless a specific constraint requires a different approach.

## Examples of the work

- [DC Reentry Housing Project]({{ reentry.url | relative_url }}) is working with a coalition to define a shared data platform for reentry housing coordination.
- [Daria]({{ daria.url | relative_url }}) grew from an abortion fund's shared-spreadsheet workflow into open-source case-management software used by multiple funds.
- [Clean Slate]({{ clean_slate.url | relative_url }}) paired legal-clinic expertise and human review with a limited eligibility-triage tool.

These cases differ in audience, maturity, and outcome. They show why user access, partner participation, privacy, maintenance, and honest status matter as much as code.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Civic Tech DC's project support free?

Selected projects receive no-cost, volunteer-led support. There is no consulting fee, but partners contribute time, expertise, user access, decisions, and continuity. Some projects may have outside costs for hosting, data, services, accessibility work, security review, or long-term maintenance.

### Do I need to know what technology to build?

No. A well-understood problem is often a better starting point than a predetermined solution. We may find that research, a process change, an existing tool, or a small data intervention is more useful than new software.

### Do I need a nonprofit or confirmed users before I contact you?

No. Individuals and early project teams can start the conversation. Civic Tech DC may help look for a validating partner or users, but a project will not move into development until there is a credible way to test the need with people close to it.

### Does the project have to be in Washington, DC?

No. We are rooted in the DMV and value local relationships, but we may support work with broader public benefit when the users, partner participation, scope, and volunteer team make sense.

### What if my proposal is not selected?

Not every useful idea fits the current mission, timing, or volunteer capacity. We will be candid about what is missing when we can. We may suggest more discovery, a smaller first step, an existing resource, another organization, or a later conversation.

### How long does a project take?

There is no standard timeline. Volunteer teams work around other responsibilities, and discovery can change the scope. If you need guaranteed delivery, emergency support, regulatory accountability, or ongoing managed services, Civic Tech DC is unlikely to be the right provider.

### Will the work be open source?

Reusable software and technical work are expected to be open source. Sensitive personal data is not published, and a public repository does not remove the need for privacy, security, legal, and ethical safeguards. We discuss those constraints before development.
