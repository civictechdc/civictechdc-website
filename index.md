## Turn a public-interest problem into an open-source project

People come to Civic Tech DC with a problem, an idea, a dataset, or an early build. We help find the people who experience the problem, validate what would be useful, scope feasible work, and—when a project is a fit and a volunteer team can form—build and iterate in the open. Selected projects receive no-cost, volunteer-led technical and data support.

## How the model works

1. **Bring the need.** Someone shares a public-interest problem, idea, workflow, dataset, or early build.
2. **Validate it with real people.** We look for users, partner organizations, and subject-matter expertise instead of assuming a solution.
3. **Scope a feasible project.** Together, we define the public benefit, open-source approach, responsibilities, and a useful first milestone.
4. **Form a volunteer team.** If the project is a fit and there is enough leadership and capacity, we introduce it at a Project Night and work in the open.

Not every proposal becomes a Civic Tech DC project, and volunteer support is not a substitute for urgent, guaranteed, or ongoing commercial services. The [project intake page]({{ site.baseurl }}/pitch) explains fit, expectations, and what happens after the first conversation.
