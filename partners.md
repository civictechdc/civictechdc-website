Civic Tech DC builds **with** partners and users. Organizations and community members contribute the context, relationships, subject-matter knowledge, feedback, and continuity that a volunteer technical team cannot supply on its own.

If you have a public-interest problem, idea, dataset, workflow, or early build, the [Start a project page]({{ site.baseurl }}/pitch) is the single place to begin. This page explains the other roles that make projects and the broader community possible.

## Partnership roles

<ul class="ctdc-partner-paths usa-list usa-list--unstyled">
  <li class="ctdc-partner-path">
    <h3 class="ctdc-partner-path__title">Problem owner</h3>
    <p class="ctdc-partner-path__body">
      Brings a concrete public-interest need, explains the current workflow,
      connects the team with users, makes or facilitates decisions, and remains
      involved through validation and handoff.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li class="ctdc-partner-path">
    <h3 class="ctdc-partner-path__title">Validating user</h3>
    <p class="ctdc-partner-path__body">
      Experiences the problem directly or supports people who do. Reviews
      assumptions, prototypes, language, data definitions, and whether the work
      is actually useful and safe.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li class="ctdc-partner-path">
    <h3 class="ctdc-partner-path__title">Subject-matter collaborator</h3>
    <p class="ctdc-partner-path__body">
      Brings legal, policy, research, program, data, accessibility, security, or
      community expertise to discovery and product decisions without
      necessarily owning the whole project.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li class="ctdc-partner-path">
    <h3 class="ctdc-partner-path__title">Event host</h3>
    <p class="ctdc-partner-path__body">
      Co-hosts a Project Night, workshop, panel, hackathon, or learning event;
      offers accessible space; or brings together a community around a useful
      public-interest topic.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li class="ctdc-partner-path">
    <h3 class="ctdc-partner-path__title">Community connector</h3>
    <p class="ctdc-partner-path__body">
      Introduces users, practitioners, volunteers, organizations, or
      communities that should be in the same conversation. Not every
      partnership needs to become a software project.
    </p>
  </li>
  <li class="ctdc-partner-path">
    <h3 class="ctdc-partner-path__title">Sponsor or in-kind supporter</h3>
    <p class="ctdc-partner-path__body">
      Helps keep events and volunteer-led work free through funding, food,
      space, compute, services, or other practical support.
    </p>
  </li>
</ul>

## What project partners can expect

- **A candid fit conversation.** Civic Tech DC may recommend more discovery, a smaller scope, an existing tool, another organization, or no project.
- **Shared decisions.** Partners and users help define the need and review the work; volunteers do not replace domain knowledge or accountable ownership.
- **Open work with appropriate safeguards.** Reusable code and technical work should be open source, while sensitive data and private information stay protected.
- **Volunteer pace and capacity.** Teams work around other responsibilities. Participation does not guarantee a deadline, finished product, or ongoing managed service.
- **A plan for what comes next.** Projects should identify maintenance, governance, handoff, or responsible retirement before those questions become emergencies.
