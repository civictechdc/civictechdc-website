We became a 501(c)(3) nonprofit in 2022. We use open-source technology to support civic engagement, strengthen democracy, and empower public-interest initiatives in the DMV and beyond.
{:.ctdc-lead}

We welcome designers, product managers, project managers, communicators, policy thinkers, researchers, and community leaders, as well as technologists, from all backgrounds, disciplines, and levels of experience. Our work supports local and national nonprofits, think tanks, local governments, and grassroots organizers across the region.

## How We Work

We gather to discuss ideas, collaborate on projects, and get things done.

<div class="ctdc-about-grid">
  <div class="ctdc-about-card">
    <strong class="ctdc-about-card__title">Twice-Monthly Project Nights</strong>
    <p>Community-led collaboration on active civic tech projects.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="ctdc-about-card">
    <strong class="ctdc-about-card__title">Code &amp; Commit Nights</strong>
    <p>Focused work sessions on project delivery.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="ctdc-about-card">
    <strong class="ctdc-about-card__title">Ongoing Collaboration</strong>
    <p>Asynchronous coordination via Slack and GitHub.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="ctdc-about-card">
    <strong class="ctdc-about-card__title">Special Events</strong>
    <p>Hackathons, workshops, show &amp; tells, and happy hours.</p>
  </div>
</div>

We believe in building processes, not just tools, and applying human-centered systems thinking to civic technology. Our projects aim to be adaptable and reusable across communities.
